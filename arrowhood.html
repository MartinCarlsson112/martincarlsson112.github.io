Project overview

High Concept. 

Development took place over 4 weeks for the second game project course at Future Games. For this course we were challenged to combine two or more sports, we were also given some limitations to choose from. 

The team consisted of 13 people. We worked in unreal engine. I mainly worked in c++, while others worked in blueprints. 

The Game

Arrowhood is a 2v2 arena archery game with parkour elements. The game takes inspiration from Quake and Overwatch. 

The goal of the playersâ€™ is to score points through capturing a crown. Capture is done by holding the crown for 15 seconds. The opponent players can prevent the capture by shooting the crown bearer.

The players can shoot arrows, charge up to simulate drawing the bow. The player can also pick up special arrows which have special effects when shot.

The game features a targeting system to help the players aim. Due to controllers, players had on average a 5% hit rate, in part due to the "spammy" nature of earlier iterations of the bow. The targeting system takes a lot of inspiration from Zelda: Breath of the Wild, where the player can compensate in any direction while they are targeting an enemy.
